2022 New Year's Resolutions

Hannah

2022-02-08 mental health badminton lists

System

In past years I’ve had mixed success with New Year’s Resolutions. A resolution gave me the motivation to move to Paris — I’ve also had years where I achieved nothing I set out to do.

This year there’s things I really want to achieve:

  • I want to be mentally healthier
  • I want to get better at badminton
  • I want to enjoy exercising

So with that in mind, I want to make sure I don’t drop off this year! I’m putting together a system to help me stay motivated, know what I need to do, and hopefully actually do it:

  1. Each resolution gets a monthly goal until it’s done, at which point I get to cross it off!
  2. I shared this list with two of my close friends and asked one of them to keep me accountable
  3. I’ll also update this page every month, crossing off completed goals and putting up the new ones — belt and braces accountability

Resolutions

  1. Go to therapy
  2. Be an A-group badminton player
  3. Practice meditation regularly
  4. Reach 100 rides on Peloton1
  5. Be hopeful and be scared

January

  1. Call the number to book therapy
  2. Ask what I need to do to get into A group
  3. Finish a 10-day meditation course on Headspace
  4. Do a CDE New Wave ride
  5. Read Feel The Fear and Do It Anyway

February

  1. Complete my course of therapy
  2. Start badminton coaching
  3. Reach 60 days of meditation
  4. Reach 35 rides
  5. Be bad at something new2

March

I finished my course of therapy on the 28th, and personally I’ve found it very helpful. Unfortunately I missed a coaching session at the end of February and didn’t manage to come up with a badminton goal for the month, which is why it’s quite generic.

  1. Stick to the coaching plan
  2. Practice loving-kindness meditation
  3. Try out three new instructors’ classes
  4. Don’t apologise for myself3

  2. I don’t necessarily have to be bad at the new thing I try: this is about doing something new despite the fear that I may suck at it. [return]
