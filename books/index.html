Books

Since 2020 I’ve kept notes on books I read. It helps me pay more attention to what I’m reading, and is a good resource when I’m trying to come up with recommendations for friends.

I use a “three-sentence summary” method that I picked up from Atomic Habits, with a pretty loose definition of sentence. For technical books I summarise each chapter too.

The Book of Five Rings

Nov 9, 2021 philosophy non-fiction

In many areas of life, but particularly martial arts, an overemphasis is placed on technique, showmanship and equipment. The martial arts that Musashi1 teach are simple, direct and practical.

Walden

non-fiction environment philosophy

Unfortunately I’ve lost my notes for the last two chapters of Walden. I’m putting this incomplete summary up for now, hopefully I’ll be able to dig out the rest.

Tomorrow Sex Will Be Good Again

non-fiction verso

Katherine Angel posits that consent is not enough, that while positive consent is the “least bad standard” for sexual assault legislation, it isn’t equipped to make “sex good again”.

Toki Pona: The Language Of Good

non-fiction toki pona

Sonja Lang introduces a microlanguage structured around minimalism and mindful awareness of the present. As an educational text, the quality of exercises is really high and the ideas are expressed clearly.

The Tombs Of Atuan

earthsea fiction

A young girl is taken from her home and stripped of her name and identity to serve the Nameless Gods of the Kargish empire.

The Mysterious Affair At Styles

poirot fiction

While visiting the home of some friends, Mr. Hastings not only witnesses a murder by poisoning but also runs into his friend, the acclaimed Belgian detective Poirot.

The Murder on the Links

poirot fiction

Poirot and Hastings, along with an arrogant Parisian detective, investigate the murder of a rich man under seemingly familiar circumstances. The solution lies in recalling this infamous crime and unravelling the man’s son’s love life.

The Farthest Shore

earthsea fiction

A young prince, heir to a long-empty throne, comes to the island of mages with word that magic is failing. He and the archmage set out across the sea to investigate, finding at the edges of the world the fatal consequences of mages’ misled desires to cheat death.

The Emancipated Spectator

non-fiction verso philosophy

The book is a ‘critique of’ critique of the spectacle, described as the belief that the bombardment of images and spectacles has rendered democractic subjects passive and ignorant.

The Art of Learning

non-fiction philosophy

Summary The book is partly an overview of Waitzkin’s successful careers as a high-level chess player and martial artist, partly a philosophical treatise on the nature of learning.

Terminal Boredom

fiction short stories

This is a selection of science fiction short stories published in Japan between the 1960s and 1980s. Where I can find a publishing year I’ve included it.

Rivers Of London

fiction

A newly-minted PC encounters a ghost while doing grunt work on a murder case, and soon becomes an apprentice in the magical wing of the Metropolitan Police.

Happy

Capitalism And The Sea

non-fiction verso

In Marx’s conception of industrial capitalism as Exploitative and alienating production of goods The realisation of value through trade and the resulting accumulation of capital the sea is both a site of production (fishing) and a medium for circulation (shipping).

non-fiction verso

Luddism has been unfairly painted as technophobic; in reality it identified that technology is a political tool used to control and exploit labour, that can and should be resisted.

earthsea fiction

A young man discovers his knack for magic when he saves the people of his village from an invading force. He is sent to a school of magic, but in his arrogance and haste to prove himself summons a dark shadow of his own power which sets itself against him.
